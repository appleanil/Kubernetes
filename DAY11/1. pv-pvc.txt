PV and PVC
==========

1️⃣ PV First
👉 Actual storage is created

2️⃣ PVC Next
👉 Requests storage from PV

3️⃣ Mongo Pod
👉 Uses PVC
👉 Starts only when PVC is Bound

4️⃣ WebApp Pod
👉 Connects to Mongo service


--> Pod(mongodb) is connected to PVC, then create PVC, also pod in pending, then create PV.
--> Pod is directly connected with PVC, not with PV. If u have PV but pod cannot connect to PV without PVC.
--> Pod and PV is there and PVC applied then firstly go with the PV bcz of access modes, requests, capacity and storage.
--> Even though PV is avaiable and Pod, PVC also not bounding then access modes(RWX,RWO) and capacity are mismatched.
--> We can "kubectl describe pod <pod-name>"  --- reason it shows bcz there is no PVC.
--> Again "kubectl describe pvc <pvc-name>"   --- reason its shows bcz thers is no PV.
--> PVC is connected to PV
--> Then create PV yaml, so that PV is bound with PVC, PVC is bound with pod

PV (storage)
   ↓
PVC (request)
   ↓
Pod (uses PVC)


1.Correct Explanation (Interview Perfect Answer) Scenario-Based Answer
----------------------------------------------------------------------

In Kubernetes, storage binding works between PV and PVC. If a Pod is created before the PVC is bound to a PV, the Pod will remain in Pending state. 
When we describe the Pod, it shows that the PVC is not available. Then, describing the PVC shows that no matching PV is available. 
Once we create a PV that satisfies the PVC request, it gets bound, and the Pod starts running.
Binding between PV and PVC is label-less but based on storage class, access modes, and capacity matching.


2.Final Correct Statement (Memorize This)
-----------------------------------------

“If PVC is created without an available PV, it remains in Pending state, which causes the Pod to also stay in Pending. 
Once a matching PV is created, it binds automatically and the Pod starts running.”

  name: mongodb-pvc  #Must match the mongodb-pvc spec, containers, volumes Claimname should be same
